Motivations of the Northern Triangle Migrants

1st. Economic reasons (low income, lack of work, etc.)

2nd. Violence or insecurity where you live

3rd. Reunite with family already living in the United States






background.

For more than a decade, migration has been happening from Central America to the United States. El Salvador, Honduras, and Guatemala, usually called as Northern Triangle, are countries that have been put into spotlight for its migration phenomenon. People migrate for various motivations. Based on Abuelafia et. al. (2019), there are at least three main reasons why people decided to migrate.


The first reason is economic reasons. People decided to do the migration because of their low income and lack of employment. By moving to the United States, migrants believe that they can get more opportunities to work and increase their income. Then, they send their salary to their home country as remittances.


The second main reason is violence or insecurity. High rate of crime and low protection to people's safety in the country of origin push people to move to another country in search for a better security.


The last reason of primary motivations why people decided to move is family already living in the United States. After a few people migrated from a household, the rest of family might want to follow them. This usually happens when children want to reunite with their parents who already migrated before them.



Since economic reasons are the first primary drive of migration, this research decided to dig deeper into remittances sent by people who have already migrated.

It can be seen that the data is dispersed and not aglommerated into a single line.



However, the data circles tend to be near the x axis. This might mean that the amount of remittances will not change significantly, even though people have been receiving it for years.
